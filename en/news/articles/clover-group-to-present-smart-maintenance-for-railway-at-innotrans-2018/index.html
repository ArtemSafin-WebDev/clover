Clover Group to present Smart Maintenance for Railway at InnoTrans 2018
Smart Maintenance, Clover PMM
28 August 2018
Smart MaintenanceClover PMM

At InnoTrans 2018, the Clover Group will present the Smart Maintenance business solution for risk-based asset management using the example of the railway industry. The solution will be introduced by a joint booth of LocoTech LLC and 2050. DIGITAL Group. 

Over the four days of the exhibition, the company will demonstrate how the solution works using real data from the LocoTech LLC. Smart Maintenance is based on the technology of estimating the real and forecast technical condition of locomotives. An optimized repair schedule is built on the basis of mathematical algorithms built into the system. Smart Maintenance allows the user to manage a depot’s repair workload and develop an optimized work schedule. 

At 14.00 on September 19, will be held an open presentation "Digitalization of the railway industry. From designing to servicing locomotives with artificial intelligence". Location — a joint stand with LocoTech LLC: CityCube, Hall A, stand 505.

For appointing business meetings with Clover Group at InnoTrans contact Anna Odintsova, Head of the Clover Group’s Communications Department, odintsova.anna@clover.global

The Clover Group is a technology leader in developing solutions based on predictive analytics for the railway, energy, engineering, and oil and gas industries. It is included into 2050. DIGITAL Group perimeter. In Russia, the company’s best-known project is Smart Locomotive, which determines the current technical condition of LocoTech’s 6,400 locomotive sections and forecasts their future condition.

LocoTech LLC manages assets which enable maintenance, repair, modernization and leasing of locomotives, production of nodes and parts for railway machinery facilities. Perimeter of managed assets includes LocoTech-Service, Zheldorremmash, LocoTech-Leasing, LocoTech Torgoviy Dom, LocoTech Promservice and so on. Production capacities consist of 10 locomotive repair facilities and about 90 service depots across Russia. Consolidated proceeds of managed assets in 2017 according to Russian Accounting Standards increased by 14% up to 105.2B rubles in comparison to metrics of 2016.
