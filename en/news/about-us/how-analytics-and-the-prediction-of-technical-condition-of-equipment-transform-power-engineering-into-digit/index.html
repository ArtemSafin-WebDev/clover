Intellectual Power Engineering: how the prediction of technical condition of equipment transform power engineering into “digit”
Technologies, Power Engineering
28 September 2017
TechnologiesPower Engineering
With the help of AI systems — intelligent analytics — energy companies are obtaining the possibility to analyze all the collected data and predict the technical condition of the equipment. One of the solutions for intelligent diagnosis and predictive analysis is Clover PMM (Predictive Maintenance & Monitoring) — the product of the Russian company Clover Group.

Tasks, solved with the help of Clover PMM in Power Engineering 

There are two modules at the heart of Clover PMM: a module for searching anomalies in operation of equipment and predicting module that builds a predict of failure for structural elements of equipment.

The first module performs intelligent equipment diagnostics and allows detecting abnormal conditions in real time. For example, Clover revealed temperature excesses in the stator slots of a turbine generator for one generating company. The excess itself was uncritical, but the system finds and outputs such cases automatically. Secondly, the system understands that this kind of situations will lead to equipment failure, especially if the operation continues in high-load modes. Therefore, the system always shows this to the user — this is intellectual diagnostics.

The predictive analysis module forecasts the state of the aggregates and is constantly trained when receiving the feedback from engineers and technologists. This module allows you to understand the state of structural elements for a certain time before deciding whether to repair or recycle the equipment.

For example, according to the plan it is necessary to put the unit in repair in six months. Prior to this, Clover has already found excess temperatures and building the prediction now — the results of anomalous behavior: it will be a defect or failure, when this event will occur — within six months or later. Thus, predicting helps to decide on the fate of equipment and to form a repair plan based on the actual and predicted state. In addition, engineers and technologists train Clover PMM with the help of machine learning.

The main task solved by Clover PMM in the power-engineering sector is the correct formation of the repair program in relation to the current and predicted technical condition of the equipment.

Users of Clover PMM

Since Clover PMM knows how to predict stop, break, find pre-failure conditions and displays this information on the computer screen, the operational personnel that manages the changes and modes are the main users. For example, the service of the chief engineer who areresponsible for the operation of the equipment. It is important for them to ensure the reliability of equipment and the continuity of its operation.

For repair and service companies, Clover PMM is convenient for planning the material and technical resources and the composition of repair teams for the power plant. Clover allows you to see the current and predicted technical condition for each unit, main and auxiliary, thereby creating the program for condition based maintenance.

Stages and timing of the implementation of intellectual analytics

The analysis is based on data, the main sources of which are TPACS, ERP, MES, MRO, any other systems storing information from sensors, information on repairs; external factors (humidity, temperature, etc.). The data are always transferred to Clover Group specialists in order to be prepared for analysis: structuring and enrichment.

It is important to understand that the accuracy of the prediction depends on the amount of data. If the customer has accumulated a sufficient amount of data, the predictive analysis module will immediately start working and it will be possible to receive the results immediately. If the data is not enough, then the information collection begins only after the implementation of the Clover PMM, and the adjustment of the predictive model will take several months.

For each equipment, you need a different amount of data. For example, for Atlas Copco compressor it was enough to get data for one year. After the data is collected, it is uploaded to Clover Expert Analytical System, where mathematicians, in cooperation with industry experts, identify abnormal conditions and defects in the operation of equipment, identify violations of operating conditions and build the predictive model of possible failures.

Industry experts and engineers train the system. Thus, it digitizes the experience of experts and applies it in analysis. For example, if a structural component breaks down or an anomaly appears, it is possible to classify the cause of the event, for example, due to a violation of the operating mode. This requires knowledge of experts; they explain what caused the anomaly, and mathematicians write this rule and upload into the system, teaching it based on expert opinions.

To output the results, Clover PMM integrates with the customer’s IT infrastructure, for example, ERP, EAM. Information on the pre-failure conditions of the equipment, data on the actual and predictive technical condition of the structural element of equipment are transferred to the system. If there is no repair management system, we integrate Clover into another system — it depends on the features of the customer’s infrastructure.



Demonstration of Clover PMM interface for the air blower

PMM is a system for big data analysis that allows you to extract value as physical dependencies. You can make decisions about the equipment based on this information. This is not a classical TP ACS system, the main function of which is to control the equipment by the target values of the parameters. These systems complement each other well: TP ACS gives data, and Clover PMM analyzes them.

Effects and benefits for the power engineering companies

The greatest benefit will be obtained by generating companies — those who are directly facing repairs and whose economy depends on the operability of the equipment of the enterprise. For example, Clover PMM will help avoid penalties due to emergency shutdowns of the units.

Short-term benefits you will be able to see since the first days of work — optimization of planning in the sector of ordering necessary spare parts and materials for equipment repairs, reducing the number of unscheduled repairs, reducing the costs for repairing equipment, monitoring the violations of operating modes.

The key long-term effect is the change in repair standards — condition based maintenance and this is an increase in the reliability of equipment and its “understanding”.
