Internet of things in the service of carsharing and industry
IoT, Industry
20 December 2017
IoTIndustry
Internal competition in the carsharing market forces the company to introduce additional services that increase the loyalty of its customers. For example, together with Hertz, one of the largest car rental companies in the world, we have implemented a seamless technical support service for users in the seven largest countries of Europe for their carsharing service 24/7. Hertz cars (both cars and small cargo models for the transport of goods) were connected to our IoT platform using telematic SIM cards. Thus, we guarantee that the customer who took the car, for example, in Italy, will not be left without help in the emergency road situation that has occurred with him on the roads of UK, regardless of whether he has access to communication or not.

IoT — Internet of things — the concept of a computer network of physical objects (“things”), equipped with built-in technologies to interact with each other or with an external environment, considering the organization of such networks as a phenomenon capable of changing economic and social processes, eliminating the need for human participation from certain actions and operations.

IoT solutions in the transport industry of Russia

Currently, the share of transport companies’ expenses for IoT solutions in total IT expenditures is 16%, and the total volume of the IoT-market in the transport sector is 13.1 billion rubles (in a significant amount, while it is generated by systems of automotive telematics, they make up about 44% of the total the current number of all M2M connections). We believe that by 2020, about 34% of the total Enterprise market of the Internet of things will be accounted for by the transport industry. Speaker: Daria Abramova, Marketing Communications Director, Orange Business Services in Russia and the CIS.

Internet of Things inIndustry

Speaker: Denis Kasimov, CEO of Clover Group

The Internet of things is just beginning to grow in different industries, and this is the right way, since production facilities are the source of huge data sets obtained from control systems (APCS, ToR), sensors on equipment, external environment. Including predicting failures of production equipment, which is a critical task for maintaining the operability of facilities, reducing unscheduled repairs and full transition to repair “on the state”.
Artificial intelligence technologies allow extracting the full value from these data and it is necessary for making decisions on the management of the plant. For example, to anticipate breakdowns, to reduce downtime of equipment and to reduce the number of unscheduled repairs.

The manufacturing companies now using intellectual analysis systems, which allows them to build a model of the technical state and predict the state of equipment. The amount and quality of downloaded data depends on whether the system finds the cause of the failures, the failure time, the specific unit with the defect. Especially relevant is the intelligent analytics for power engineering, machine building, oil and gas industry, service companies.

As an example, we can show you one major energy company (we cannot disclose the name), which revealed a systematic excess of the temperature of the stator winding for 18 months with the help of intellectual analytics. The breakdown of the insulation of the turbine generator became the reason of excess of the temperature. In case of determination of this trend in advance, it would be possible to prevent breakage. Another bright example is the project “Smart Locomotive” in engineering industry. The anomaly search module now works and the system finds failures in the operation of equipment and violations of operating modes of locomotives. The expert technologists with the help of algorithms of machine learning are continuously training the system. The experts set the rules for the operation of units, and predicts become more accurate with each passing day. The Internet of things and artificial intelligence is already here and work for industries.
