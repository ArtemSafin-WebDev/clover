Denis Kasimov: “Industrialists began to understand the benefits of predicting the breakdowns of equipment”
Technologies
5 September 2017
Technologies
— How did the concept of repair of industrial equipment develop in historical retrospect? What made it possible to predict the breakdown of industrial equipment?

Remembering the history, it is clear that at the beginning of the 20th century, something that had already broken down was being repaired. Later, they began to carry out preventive repairs (in the Soviet terminology — PPM, planned preventive maintenance), and finally — repairs on the actual state, with predicting and prevention of breakdowns. Now it is clear that there will not be special magic technology, which will make it possible to predict the breakdown of industrial equipment.

It is necessary to develop the large pool of technologies and techniques. And the first necessary condition is sufficient computer power which makes it possible to process and analyze large amounts of information. The second condition is the necessary level of development of mathematical algorithms. The third component is the mental readiness of customers and society to apply the technology of predictive data analysis for industrial equipment. And finally, the fourth necessary condition for the introduction of the predictive analysis of the state of equipment and its condition based maintenance — is a sufficiently high level of automation of production, involving the use of modern equipment equipped with telemetric sensors and SCADA systems.

— When and how did you come up with the idea of creating the platform for the intellectual analysis of industrial data? What is the background of the development of the platform?

I had an idea for a long time — ten years ago, when I was still a student of Kazan Power Engineering University. Simultaneously with my studies, I actively worked in consulting, in the field of hydropower and metallurgy, implementing the RCM methodology (Reliability-Centered Management, reliability and safety management of equipment). One of my tasks as a consultant was to come to the production site, find out what equipment breakdown happens more often, how the equipment is diagnosed, in order to systematize the knowledge, then put it into a certain methodology. Then I became convinced that the diagnostics at the factory was most often carried out on an intuitive level, literally — an experienced specialist puts the palm on the compressor, and determine how the bearings work.

Then I realized that there is a big gap between the production workers, and those who work in managing companies, which are most often located in Moscow. This led to untimely repair, increased accident rate. And one of the tragic evidence for this was the accident at the Sayano-Shushenskaya hydroelectric power station in 2009, when the technical state of the hydroelectric unit No. 2 was not observed. At the same time, at the university, I studied the course on neural networks, and realized that the neural network the technology that you need to use to predict and repair the state. As a result, I wrote a thesis on assessing the technical state of a transformer based on technology using neural networks. At that time I did not yet have managerial experience, then I accumulated it for eight years and two years ago I realized that I could create a business.

— Let’s talk about the competitive environment in which your business exists. Is there a segment of solutions of this class on the Russian IT market? What is it — the main players, leading products?

In the form of the segment, there is no such class on the Russian market, today it is just emerging, and it can be metaphorically called the “blue ocean”, open to all players. So far, only a few companies have created products for predicting the technical condition of equipment, products capable of reaching this open ocean. Naturally, such giants as SAP, IBM, Yandex and a number of other vendors are growing in this direction; some of them already have relevant specialized products in their portfolio.

— Are there products from large vendors from the west in our market?

They are presented, but there are no completed implementations, as far as I know. By the way, the global IT market in this area is not far ahead of the Russian market in its development.

Why is the birth and formation of a software segment for the predictive analysis of the state of industrial equipment taking place right now? Is this related to the current state of the Russian economy?

The formation of this segment of software is definitely connected with the current state of the Russian economy. However it is more influenced by the global trend of digitalization of the economy. The trend towards digitalization has become evident to everyone, including most major Russian enterprises. There was a corresponding change in business models.

Manufacturers realized the economic benefits of implementing and using PMM (Predictive Maintenance & Monitoring) systems that reduce the cost of equipment maintenance, ensure reliability and continuity of production, which is especially important, for example in the energy sector. In general, it is obvious that systems using neural networks for analyzing equipment are the major elements of a large digital world in which we will live.

— What is the Clover PMM module — the architecture, the main components?

One of the modules of Clover platform is Clover PMM (Predictive Maintenance & Monitoring), which consists of three blocks. The lowest of them is the level of ETL (Extract Transform Load), which contains everything related to uploading, storing, processing data. Including distributed computing and data storage. This level is developed in Java and Scala. The next level of the module is mathematical models, logic, algorithms of calculations. The programming language is Python. And the third component is the level of visualization, in which information, obtained as a result of data analysis, is presented in a convenient and user-friendly form. Developed in JavaScript. As a DBMS, we use Apache Cassandra.

— At what stage of development is the Clover platform now? Is it a completed commercial product that has already been introduced to the market? Are there any implementations?

The Clover platform is marketed as a commercial product. In our portfolio there are both commercial implementations and pilot projects. At the same time, the platform still cannot do everything. A full-featured version of the platform is in the process of finalization, since the system is not yet able to solve equally well all tasks, analyze and predict the repair of any type of equipment.

— A variety of types of production and equipment are represented in industry. Does the Clover platform have a sector focus — for example, maybe it is oriented towards equipment in power engineering or transport sector?

The platform is able to work in any industrial field, with any equipment, and in this respect it is universal. Universality is provided by special mathematical modeling for each type of production and equipment. Presence of the preliminary stage of setting up a mathematical model is a prerequisite for implementing the platform; otherwise the technology will not work. This is not a boxed solution.

— What are the main production tasks that can be solved on the basis of the platform?

The first task is the intellectual diagnostics of industrial equipment. Typically, the operator monitors the values of the equipment parameters, ensuring that they are in the nominal range indicated by the manufacturer for each parameter. However, this approach does not take into account the internal relationships between the parameters.

The Intellectual analysis based on use of the special mathematical apparatus allows you to identify hidden anomalous modes of operation of equipment. The second important task is to determine which of the equipment nodes will break, and the detailing is important — when it will break, why it will break, under what operating modes it will break. This is the prediction of its breakdown.

— What information systems are the data sources for you? Do the problems of integration arise with domestic or Western systems?

In general, the sources are two types of systems: a low-level SCADA system and accounting systems. From the first system we take the data of the telemetric sensors of the equipment, from the second we take the data about the fixed defects, the repairs carried out and so on. In sum, this information allows you to predict the breakdown. Problems in terms of integration with information systems do not arise. We have examples of integration, “1C”, also with products of Western vendors and self-written solutions.

— Outline the range of key potential customers of your solution?

Potential customers of our platform are asset-intensive enterprises of any industry with a large fleet of equipment that must be effectively managed. These are representatives of the Enterprise segment, according to business terminology.

— Is the platform interesting and affordable for SMB segment enterprises?

Potentially, I think it is interesting, but at the current stage of market development, this will be an expensive solution for SMB segment enterprises.

— What is the interest of service companies servicing equipment on contractual terms? For the equipment manufacturers?

The service company guarantees to its customer that the repaired equipment will work during the warranty period. It is very important for such companies to understand correctly how the equipment works, to predict when it will break down. Some service companies have already become our customers. For equipment manufacturers feedback on the operation of their equipment is important in order to improve its parameters and increase reliability.

— Tell us about the project, in which the platform is implemented by the service company?

Last year we won a tender for a project for the service company “Locomotive Technologies” (LLC LocoTech), which serves the locomotive fleet of Russian Railways. This project became one of our first major implementations. It started in December 2016, and now it is in a very active stage. LokoTech has 92 service depots in various regions of the country where 11 locomotive series are serviced. Today in 20 depots, 4 series of locomotives are on trial operation as a project for the implementation of our platform.

— What happens during the trial operation phase? When will be the transition to the industrial stage?

At the stage of experimental operation, experts receive information from locomotive sensors when train staged into depot. Then they upload the data to our system, and it shows specialists where problems can arise and where to look carefully. In September, the second part of the project will be implemented, when the system will predict which node will break within the next hundred hours. The project is planned to be completed by the end of this year. The Industrial operation will start by the beginning of next year. Judging by the publications in the press, this will be the first project in Russia, in which a system of this class was put into industrial operation.

— What other interesting projects are there in the company’s portfolio?

In the transport sector, the project on electric trains, similar to the one I have already described, will soon be launched. In addition, one of our customers is a foreign compressor manufacturer, which has a representative office in Russia. Compressors are on their full service, they guarantee almost continuous operation of their equipment, and the manufacturer takes all the risks associated with the breakdown. Therefore, the company is very interested in using our solution.

In the pilot phase of the project, we predicted that one of the compressors would shut down after three weeks due to temperature fluctuations in the cooling system, which helped the vendor to prevent its emergency shutdown. Another example — at one of the hydroelectric power stations, we analyzed the information about generator’s operation for two years and saw that it was possible to detect a malfunction in its starter 1.5 years before the generator was burned out. And thus prevent its breakdown, as well as the cost of overhaul, which amounted to about 150 million rubles.

— Tell us about your company: organizational structure, branches, number of employees, their specialization?

The headquarters of our company is located in Kazan, there is a branch in Moscow, the staff of about 70 people. The core of the team is data scientists who build mathematical models, industry experts who understand the operation of equipment and the physics of processes very well. Next are the developers, analysts, project managers and specialists who are the members of teams for different projects.

— What are the plans for further development and promotion of the Clover platform for the current year and for the next two or three years?

Our main goal for the near future is to fill the Clover platform with mathematical models with the aim of making it possible to perform analysis of almost any industrial equipment as quickly as possible. The second important direction of the platform development is the lowering of the entrance threshold for developing new solutions based on our platform. At some point, we would like to transfer the implementation to our partners — system integrators, and to work only with the development of the platform. This is important for us as a software vendor.

— At what stage does the network of partners exist today?

There are a few partners who are developing the industrial Internet market in our country in cooperation with us. For example, among the partners there are consulting companies. After all, it is important not only to provide the business with values, but also important to know how to apply it — for example, to change the logistics of equipment repair in accordance with new opportunities in terms of accurate breakdown prediction. Consultants and system integrators can help in this matter.
